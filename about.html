the story

why we built it

we started tono after watching a friend spend fifteen minutes crafting a two-sentence text to a landlord — rephrasing it, asking us if it sounded too aggressive, rewriting again, deleting it all, starting over.

the problem wasn't language. it wasn't grammar. it was tone — that thing you only hear in your head, that other people only hear from the words on the screen, and that nothing in the writing process actually helps you calibrate.

existing tools check your spelling. they fix your comma splices. they don't tell you that "per my last email" reads like a slap, or that "just checking in!" sounds chipper when you actually feel skeptical.

so we built tono: a keyboard that lives where you type, gives you a tone read before you send, and gets out of your way the second you've got it.

it's not magic. it's a small, fast coach trained on what makes messages land. and it's built by a small team that uses it every day — because we have also sent that text.
