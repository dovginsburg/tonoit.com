every message has a tone. tono gives you four ways to shape it — instantly, inside any app.

warmer

soften without losing your point

sometimes the right answer is "no" — but how you say it decides whether you burn a bridge or build one. tono rewrites sharp edges into warmth while keeping your intent intact.

  • great for pushback, rejections, and "we need to talk" moments
  • adds warmth without padding — no extra fluff, just kinder phrasing
  • preserves your meaning — your no still sounds like your no
drafted
that's a terrible idea
warmer
i'm not sure that approach will land — can we explore something else?
clearer

cut the fluff. land in one read.

the average slack message gets read in under two seconds. if your reader has to ask "wait, what?" you lost them. clearer trims hedges, jargon, and throat-clearing so your point lands the first time.

  • removes "just," "wanted to," "i think maybe" — filler that signals uncertainty
  • flattens nested clauses and "per my last email" energy
  • leads with the ask — readers don't have to scroll to find it
drafted
i was just wondering if it might be possible to maybe get the deck over whenever you get a chance?
clearer
can you send the deck by eod tomorrow?
funnier

wit when it earns its keep

not every message needs a joke — but sometimes the right light touch turns a stiff exchange into an actual conversation. funnier adds a measured dose of personality without going full try-hard.

  • adds warmth via levity — useful for deflecting awkward asks
  • never forces jokes where the message needs to be serious
  • great for social dms, group chats, and breaking the ice
drafted
hey, are you free for a call at 3?
funnier
got 30 mins at 3 if you want to hear me complain about spreadsheets?
safer

catch the things you wish you could unsend

typos are easy. it's the second-guessing that takes time — "is this too aggressive? did i just sound passive-aggressive?" safer flags the risky bits before they become a follow-up conversation you didn't want.

  • spots all-caps, sarcasm, and tone that reads sharper than you meant
  • highlights phrasing that reads passive-aggressive in writing
  • especially useful when tired, frustrated, or replying on the fly
drafted
wow ok cool. just do whatever you want.
safer
no worries — let me know what works best on your end.
